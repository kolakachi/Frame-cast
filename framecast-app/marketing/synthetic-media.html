WyvStudio Synthetic Media Policy

Last updated: 4 June 2026  ·  Effective: 4 June 2026

WyvStudio's character feature lets you upload one reference photo and reuse that likeness as a recurring on-screen character. This policy sets the rules for whose likeness may be used, what consent is required, how the resulting media must be disclosed, and how affected people can request takedowns. It is the single most-load-bearing policy on this site — every other policy refers back to it where likeness questions arise.

1. What "Synthetic Media" Means Here

For the purposes of this policy, "synthetic media" includes any video, image, audio, or character output produced or modified by WyvStudio's AI features, including:

  • AI-generated character images and scenes (gpt-image-2 character path)
  • Image-to-video animations of those characters
  • Generated voice narration over those characters
  • Composite outputs combining the above with stock visuals, music, captions, and overlays

2. The Consent Rule (the Important One)

You may only upload a reference photo of a person if (a) you are that person, (b) you have that person's documented, time-stamped, written permission to use their likeness for the purpose you are using it, or (c) the person depicted is a fictional character you own or have licensed.

When you upload a reference photo through the character flow, you confirm one of those three conditions and you confirm that the permission, if any, was given for the specific commercial or personal use you intend. If we later determine the upload did not meet that standard, we will treat it as a violation of this policy and our Acceptable Use Policy.

2.1 What "documented permission" means

For someone other than yourself, written permission must be retained and producible on request. Acceptable forms include:

  • A signed talent / model release covering AI-generated derivatives and the platforms you intend to publish on
  • A written employment or contractor agreement that grants likeness rights for marketing purposes
  • An email or chat message from the person, clearly identifying the photo and the use, where their identity is verifiable

Verbal permission alone is not sufficient because we cannot evidence it on takedown review. Permission obtained for one use (for example, a single TV ad) does not extend to other uses (for example, multiple AI-generated social videos in perpetuity) unless the document says so.

3. Strict Prohibitions on Real People

Even with consent, you may not generate synthetic media that:

  • Depicts a minor (under 18) in any sexual or sexualised context, real or synthetic. No exceptions.
  • Depicts an adult in a sexual or sexualised context without their explicit, separate written consent for that specific category. A general talent release is not enough.
  • Falsely depicts a person committing a crime, holding a position they do not hold, endorsing a product they do not endorse, or saying words they did not say — even with a release — if a reasonable viewer might believe the depiction is real.
  • Depicts violence, injury, or death of a real person.

4. Public Figures

"Public figure" means any person who is widely recognised by the public for their role — including but not limited to politicians, government officials, judges, candidates, business executives of large companies, established media personalities, athletes, musicians, actors, religious leaders, and journalists. Synthetic media of public figures presents heightened risk regardless of consent:

  • We block reference-image uploads that our classification systems match to a database of recognisable public-figure faces. Matches are quarantined and the upload is rejected with an explanation.
  • We block prompts that name public figures as the subject of an AI character generation.
  • Even where automated systems fail to detect a public figure, attempting to generate one is a violation of this policy and may result in account termination.
  • Authorised use cases (e.g. an executive generating AI ads featuring their own face) require a verified identity match. Contact hello@wyvstudio.com for the verification process.

5. Disclosure Obligations

When you publish WyvStudio outputs that depict a person — even a consenting one — to any audience outside your own organisation, you must:

  • Disclose that the content is AI-generated, using language a reasonable viewer can understand (for example, a caption that says "AI-generated content" or a platform's built-in AI-content label)
  • Comply with any applicable jurisdiction's synthetic-media disclosure law, including the EU AI Act's transparency requirements, California's SB 970 / AB 2655, and other emerging regulation
  • Honour the disclosure standards of any platform you publish on (YouTube's altered-content disclosure, TikTok's AIGC label, Meta's AI-generated content label, etc.)

6. WyvStudio's Own Labelling and Watermarking

  • Every asset produced by an AI generation step is tagged ai_generated in our internal data model and surfaces that label in the editor.
  • Free-tier exports carry a visible "Made with WyvStudio" watermark that identifies the output as AI-generated and traceable to the workspace.
  • Paid-plan exports may remove the watermark but are still tagged and traceable in our internal audit log.
  • We retain provenance metadata — workspace, user, reference asset, prompt, model — for at least 90 days for every generation, to enable takedown investigation and law-enforcement cooperation.

7. Voice Cloning

The same consent rules in §2 apply to voice cloning. You may only upload a voice sample of yourself, of a person who has given documented permission for AI-generated voice derivatives, or of a licensed fictional character. Synthetic voice in a marketing or news context that simulates a specific real person without explicit consent is prohibited and will result in immediate sanction.

8. Takedown for Nonconsensual Synthetic Media

If you are the subject of a WyvStudio-generated image, video, or voice clip created without your permission, you can request immediate removal:

  1. Email hello@wyvstudio.com with the subject line Likeness Removal.
  2. Include the URL of the offending content (a WyvStudio share link, social-platform URL, or exported asset URL) and a description of where the content appears.
  3. Include a description of the depicted person and how we can verify identity (an attached photo of yourself is helpful but not required for the initial request).
  4. Confirm under penalty of perjury that you did not authorise the synthetic media and have a good-faith belief the depiction is unauthorised.

We acknowledge requests within one business day. We act on credible requests within three business days — typically faster than the DMCA timeline because nonconsensual synthetic media presents a higher risk of ongoing harm. We do not require legal counsel or a notarised signature for these requests.

9. Cooperation with Law Enforcement and Researchers

  • We report suspected CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline and preserve evidence pursuant to applicable law.
  • We respond to valid law-enforcement requests for synthetic-media provenance, subscriber records, and account history. We require a properly issued subpoena, warrant, or equivalent international legal process before we disclose user data; we may notify the user unless the request prohibits notice.
  • We will engage in good faith with safety researchers and journalists investigating misuse of generative-AI tools, subject to user-privacy obligations.

10. Independent Audit and Improvement

We will independently audit our synthetic-media safeguards at least annually starting in the first year we exceed 1,000 paying customers. The audit will cover prompt-blocking accuracy, public-figure recognition accuracy, takedown response times, and the rate of false positives in upload moderation. We will publish a summary of findings.

11. Updates

The norms and laws around synthetic media are evolving quickly. We will revise this policy as they do. Material changes are announced via email and in-app notification at least seven days before they take effect. Continued use of the Service after the effective date constitutes acceptance.
