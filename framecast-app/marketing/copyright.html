WyvStudio Copyright & DMCA Policy

Last updated: 4 June 2026  ·  Effective: 4 June 2026

We respect the intellectual-property rights of others and expect every user of WyvStudio to do the same. This policy describes how copyright holders can submit takedown notices, how affected users can counter-notify, and the actions we take against repeat infringers. It implements the safe-harbour provisions of the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and equivalent national IP regimes.

1. Designated Agent for Copyright Claims

Send all DMCA notices and counter-notices to:

Designated Copyright Agent — WyvStudio
Kollignton Kay LTD
Email: hello@wyvstudio.com (subject line: DMCA Takedown or DMCA Counter-Notice)
Postal: Kollignton Kay LTD, United Kingdom (full registered address available on the Terms of Service)

We treat email submissions as the primary channel for speed. Postal submissions are accepted but processed on a slower timeline.

2. Filing a Takedown Notice

To be valid under §512(c)(3) of the DMCA, your notice must include all of the following:

  1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or a person authorised to act on their behalf
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed (registration number if available)
  3. Identification of the specific material on WyvStudio that is allegedly infringing, including a direct URL (a WyvStudio share link, project URL, or exported asset URL) sufficient for us to locate it
  4. Your full name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  6. A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are authorised to act on behalf of the rights holder

Incomplete notices delay our response. If your notice is materially incomplete we will email you a request to supplement it within 10 business days; otherwise we will close it without action.

3. What Happens After You File

  • Day 0–1: We acknowledge receipt by email.
  • Day 1–3: We verify the notice is facially valid and the URL points to material on our Service.
  • Day 3–5: We remove or disable access to the identified material, notify the affected user, and forward your contact information to them (as required by §512(g)).
  • Day 10–14: If the affected user submits a valid counter-notice, we forward it to you. You then have 10–14 business days to file suit for an injunction. If you do not, the material may be restored.

4. Filing a Counter-Notice

If you are a WyvStudio user and we have removed your content in response to a takedown notice, you may submit a counter-notice. A valid counter-notice under §512(g)(3) must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the material that was removed and the URL where it appeared before removal
  3. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification
  4. Your full name, address, telephone number, and email
  5. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if you reside outside the United States, the United Kingdom courts where Kollignton Kay LTD is registered), and that you will accept service of process from the complainant

We forward valid counter-notices to the original complainant. If they do not file suit within 10–14 business days, we may restore the material.

5. Misrepresentation

Under §512(f), filing a knowingly false takedown notice or counter-notice can subject you to liability for damages, including costs and attorneys' fees. We will record and may publicly identify patterns of bad-faith filings.

6. Repeat-Infringer Policy

In accordance with §512(i), we will terminate the accounts of users who are determined to be repeat infringers. Our standard is:

  • First valid notice: content removed, user warned, infringement counter incremented
  • Second valid notice within 12 months: content removed, account suspended for review
  • Third valid notice within 12 months, or any single notice for severe / criminal infringement: account terminated, workspace deleted, refunds withheld for the offending cycle

We may apply a stricter standard for accounts that show a pattern of deliberate infringement, fraudulent counter-notices, or use of WyvStudio outputs to systematically misrepresent ownership of third-party works.

7. AI-Generated Output and Reference Inputs

WyvStudio generates outputs based on prompts, reference images, and audio you upload. You are responsible for the rights you assert when uploading reference material. If you upload an image, voice sample, or other content that you do not own and do not have permission to use, the resulting AI-generated output is potentially infringing. The takedown process above applies to AI-generated outputs just as it does to direct uploads. See our Synthetic Media Policy for the consent and rights framework on reference inputs.

8. Trademark Complaints

If you believe a user is using your trademark in WyvStudio outputs in a way that creates consumer confusion or dilutes your mark, send a notice to hello@wyvstudio.com with the subject line Trademark Complaint. Include the registration number, the mark, the goods/services classes, the URL where the disputed use appears, and a brief statement of the basis for confusion. We review trademark complaints under a similar timeline to DMCA notices.

9. Right of Publicity / Likeness Complaints

If you believe your name, voice, or likeness has been used in a WyvStudio output without your consent, the synthetic-media takedown path is faster than DMCA. Email hello@wyvstudio.com with the subject line Likeness Complaint and the URL of the offending content. Our Synthetic Media Policy describes the full process.

10. Updates

We update this policy as the law evolves and as we operate at scale. Material changes are announced via email at least seven days before they take effect.
